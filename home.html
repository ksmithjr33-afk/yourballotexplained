Why this exists

Most of us walk into the booth knowing the race at the top — and guessing at everything else. But the offices you've never heard of — your county commissioner, your state rep, the railroad commissioner — often hit your rent, your roads, your schools, and your paycheck harder than the ones on TV. This makes all of it make sense — so a confused voter becomes a confident one.

Who's behind it

Built to inform — not to persuade.

I'm Kenneth Smith. I make money and civics make sense for regular people — because information you can't understand isn't really yours. This tool is a nonpartisan project: it explains your ballot and rates what each office does, but it never endorses a candidate or a party. The goal is simple — a voter who actually knows what they're voting on.

Full transparency: I also do separate political and volunteer work. This tool is walled off from it — every candidate is presented identically, the impact score rates the office (not the people), and nothing here tells you how to vote. Always confirm your specific ballot at Vote411.org.
