Why this exists

Most of us walk into the booth knowing the race at the top — and guessing at everything else. But the offices you've never heard of — your county commissioner, your state rep, the railroad commissioner — often hit your rent, your roads, your schools, and your paycheck harder than the ones on TV. And the representatives you elect are the ones who go to the House and the Senate — the people who decide which bills pass and become the laws you live under. This makes all of it make sense — so a confused voter becomes a confident one.

Questions & answers

Is it really free?

Yes. Your guide is free — no account, no catch. I just ask for a donation if it helped, because the AI that reads your ballot and builds the guide costs me real money for every one.

How accurate is it?

Your races and candidates come from your official Vote411 ballot plus a web search, so it's accurate to those sources. But AI can occasionally misread a name, so always confirm against your official ballot at Vote411.org before you vote.

Do you store my address or data?

No. Your screenshots are used only to build your guide and aren't saved anywhere. You never even type your address here — that happens on Vote411's own site.

Does it tell me who to vote for?

Never. It explains what each office does and lists your real candidates — the choice is 100% yours. It's strictly nonpartisan and always will be.

Why do some races show no candidates yet?

For elections that are still months away, candidate lists often aren't published anywhere yet. Those races fill in closer to Election Day — check back later or grab a fresh screenshot then.

What's the $15 human-reviewed guide?

I build that one by hand and verify every race myself, plus add how competitive each race is (which the free AI can't reliably determine). It's for anyone who wants guaranteed accuracy and extra detail.
