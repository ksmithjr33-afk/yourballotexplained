How the impact score works

It's a framework, not a fact — each office scored 0–100 on three things you can check yourself: proximity (does it hit your street, taxes, or family), frequency (how often you feel it), and control (one officeholder vs. one vote among many). Use it as a lens, then decide for yourself. It rates the office — never the candidates.

A note on offices

Not every state or county elects every office listed here, and some go by slightly different names (a "county commissioner" may be a "supervisor," etc.). Your Step 1 lookup shows the exact offices on your ballot.
